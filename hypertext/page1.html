I'm here to help you however I can."

As you exchange pleasantries, you open the door and step inside, Owens following after you. "We should be able to resolve this fairly quickly, I think." He leads you down a lamp-lit hallway. "Aside from the victim himself, there were only four other people in the house at the time of death: his wife, son, and two servants, his maid and secretary. It's pretty unlikely to have been an outside job. I had a man go around to every possible entrypoint in the house, and they were all secure and showed no signs of tampering."

Turning a corner at the end of the hallway reveals a door, nondescript in every way but for the officer guarding it. When he sees you and Owens, he nods and makes way for you to enter the room. "Well," says Owens, "this here is Sutherland's study, the scene of the crime. It's been untouched since we got here. Shall we?"
