The master bedroom holds a grand four-poster bed, its wooden posts carved with intricate designs. It must have cost a fortune.

A woman sits on nearby chaise lounge, reading. Her hands are decorated with golden rings with gemstones of all colors and sizes. Around her neck sits a gaudy diamond necklace. The gems glitter in the lamplight.

"This is Mrs. Rachel Sutherland, the victim's wife," Owens tells you.

Rachel looks up at you, then sets her book down. Her hands fall to her lap, resting atop crossed legs in a rather prim manner. You can't help but note that she's much younger than her husband, and that she doesn't seem very distraught by his death.

"Well?" She asks. "Do you have any questions for me?"

You ask her for her alibi at the time of the crime.

"I was in the library with Mr. Woods, Pierce's secretary. We were discussing a novel the two of us had both read recently, when we both heard a scream. Mr. Woods left to go see what happened, and I stayed behind until he returned and told me what happened. We were both still there when the police arrived."

Owens nods at this. It seems he corroborates her story.

"Is that all?" she asks with an air of finality.

You thank her for her time, and she picks up her book to resume reading.
